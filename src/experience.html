My experience along the way...

My About section already explains that ever since I was 7 years old, I began drawing scenes from cartoons and shows I used to watch as a kid. I was also influenced by internet creators and video game characters such as Sonic the Hedgehog, Cartoon Network/DisneyXD games, and Five Nights at Freddy’s. Whenever I could, I practiced and implemented more techniques like perspective, semi‑accurate tracing, and, at certain points, depth. As a result, drawing became one of my hobbies and one of the skills I practiced the most.

Years later, that same feeling carried over into video games—the feeling of controlling something on the screen and seeing how my actions could lead to positive or negative outcomes, working for me or against me, made me feel incredibly good. I went even further in middle school, starting with projects on the Scratch website and school assignments using RoboEXP. Even though those projects are still basic today, they serve as documentation of my constant growth in knowledge related to technology and interactivity.

As time went on, I achieved more things, including:

  • Junior Colegas Pfizer Program 2024 — I gave a presentation to more than 50 Pfizer employees during the summer of 2024, explaining and demonstrating the USP of the Copilot (Windows 365) tool.
  • RadioIMA, Instituto Miguel Ángel A.C. — Certificate: Introduction to Radio Production (132 hours).
  • VII National Educational Robotics Competition: Robomatrix Junior 2023 — First Place. Collaborative work in building and programming a robot. Guadalajara, Jalisco, June 2023.
  • Netica Regional Robotics Competition 2023 — First place in the Middle School–High School category: “Maze.” Mexico City, Mexico, March 2023.
  • Seasonal Work at Suburbia— Worked as part of the Suburbia team in the Hard Line area for one month, from November to December 2025.

I am currently continuing to learn new things and gaining more experience through projects related to the things I am passionate about.
