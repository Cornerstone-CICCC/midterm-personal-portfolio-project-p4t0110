A little bit about me.

My name is Patricio Daniel Caballero Garcia. I am 18 years old, born on March 23rd, 2007, in Mexico City. I spent my childhood with my mother, Maricela, along with my grandmother Lourdes. Sadly, my grandmother passed away in 2014, an event that triggered even more hardships, such as the death of my mother, living with relatives who weren’t the best, and more. But after everything, my life improved in an extraordinary way when I went to live with another aunt, Beatriz. It was during this time that I discovered my vocation and passion for programming, computer science, and above all… video games.

In middle school, I became very interested in the system interfaces we were using: RoboEXP—simple but addictive. I helped my classmates understand the topics better, so everyone assumed that was my talent. My aunt Beatriz also supported that idea, suggesting that when I grew up, I could program my own video games. That thought sparked many more ideas. When I was little, I loved drawing and creating iconic scenes related to visual art and cartoons. At the same time, I developed a huge love for music; I used to play songs in the background while working in Scratch and on various personal and school projects. The same story continued in high school, except that this time a unique opportunity appeared: participating in a robotics competition.

The first level was the regional one, held at school, using Arduino. I ended up winning the competition, which allowed me to advance to the next level: the national competition in Guadalajara, Mexico. I had more experiences in high school, such as a radio broadcasting course, which helped me with audio production and management—something that sparked an interest in another area of music: music production. Outside of school, one of my uncles invited me to a program called Colegas Jr Pfizer, where I gave a presentation about using Copilot and its benefits when working with it. All these experiences helped shape the vocation I have today: web development.

I already had experience in drawing and, to some extent, making music, so I wanted to expand my knowledge in programming—not just for video games, but also for websites, applications, and service-oriented systems for people. I love what I do, and it’s all thanks to the people who have been part of my life. Thank you all for being here :)
